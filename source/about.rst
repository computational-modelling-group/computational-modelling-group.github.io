About Southampton's Computational Modelling Group (CMG)
-------------------------------------------------------


The Computational Modelling Group brings together researchers and students from
across the University of Southampton working with computing simulation, computer
assisted data analysis and research projects that have software engineering
aspects.

Since the inception of the group in 2009, more than 1500 PhD students, research fellows and academics have been group members, and have benefitted from the networking, training opportunities and support that the community can provide

The Computational Modelling Group was founded in 2009 by Hans Fangohr with the aim to bring together all research at the university that involves computer simulation, significant computer based data processing and visualisation. The webpage (cmg.soton.ac.uk) provides a structured overview of the computational research activities, a list of selected projects, an events list capturing relevant seminars and workshops, and of course the people behind this. 

A key role of the group is to enable networking between traditionally disjoint faculties and disciplines: often the same computational problem or technique and exists in multiple discplines, but is typically known under different names. Computational training needs for PhD students and postdocs are similar in different disciplines, and the group tries to streamline this across the university. The group also demonstrates critical mass in computational modelling at Southampton to research councils, industry and other parties.

All academics, researchers and postgraduate students working with computational modelling are encouraged to join the group, please explore http://cmg.soton.ac.uk for further information and get in touch with the group's head Hans Fangohr <fangohr@soton.ac.uk> if required.
